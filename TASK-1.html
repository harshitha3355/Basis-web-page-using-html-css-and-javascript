SWEET CRAVINGS

No idea what to bake! Here are 7 different cakes you can make easily Discover the joy of baking with our guide to seven delightful cake recipes! Whether you're a novice or a seasoned baker, these recipes offer something for everyone.

Black Forest Cake

A Black Forest cake is a classic German dessert known for its layers of chocolate sponge, whipped cream, and cherries. To prepare it, begin by baking three layers of moist chocolate sponge cake. Sift together flour, cocoa powder, baking powder, and salt. In a separate bowl, beat eggs and sugar until fluffy, then fold in the dry ingredients and melted butter. Once the cakes are baked and cooled, slice each in half for thinner layers if desired. For the filling, pit fresh cherries or use canned ones, simmered with sugar and cornstarch until thick. Whip chilled heavy cream with powdered sugar and vanilla extract until stiff peaks form. To assemble, brush the layers with cherry syrup or kirsch (cherry liqueur), spread the whipped cream, and add the cherry filling. Stack the layers, covering the top and sides with more whipped cream. Garnish with chocolate shavings, whole cherries, and extra whipped cream for decoration. Refrigerate the cake for a few hours to set the flavors. The combination of the rich chocolate, sweet cherries, and light cream makes for a delightful, indulgent treat that's perfect for special occasions or simply to satisfy a sweet craving.

Chocolate Banana Cake

To make a delicious chocolate banana cake, start by preheating your oven to 350°F (175°C) and greasing a 9-inch round cake pan. In a bowl, mash three ripe bananas until smooth. Add 1/2 cup melted butter, 1 cup granulated sugar, and 2 large eggs, whisking until well combined. Stir in 1 teaspoon vanilla extract. In another bowl, sift together 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour, 1/2 cup unsweetened cocoa powder, 1 teaspoon baking soda, and a pinch of salt. Gradually fold the dry ingredients into the banana mixture, mixing until just combined. For added richness, stir in 1/2 cup chocolate chips. Pour the batter into the prepared cake pan and bake for 35-40 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean. Allow the cake to cool in the pan for 10 minutes before transferring it to a wire rack to cool completely. Optionally, top with chocolate ganache or dust with powdered sugar before serving. The cake offers a perfect blend of moist banana flavor and rich chocolate, making it an indulgent treat for any occasion. Enjoy this delightful dessert with a cup of coffee or a scoop of vanilla ice cream.

Lemon Cake

Here's a simple lemon cake recipe for a delightful, zesty treat. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a 9-inch round cake pan. In a bowl, whisk together 1½ cups of all-purpose flour, 1½ teaspoons of baking powder, and ¼ teaspoon of salt. In a separate bowl, cream ½ cup of softened butter with 1 cup of granulated sugar until light and fluffy. Beat in 2 large eggs, one at a time, then add the zest of 2 lemons and 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract. Gradually mix in the dry ingredients, alternating with ½ cup of milk, beginning and ending with the flour mixture. Pour the batter into the prepared pan and bake for 25-30 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean. Let the cake cool in the pan for 10 minutes before transferring to a wire rack. For the glaze, combine 1 cup of powdered sugar with 2 tablespoons of freshly squeezed lemon juice. Drizzle the glaze over the cooled cake, allowing it to set before serving. The result is a moist, flavorful lemon cake with a perfect balance of sweetness and tang, ideal for any occasion.

Cream Cheese Round Cake

To make a delicious cream cheese round cake, begin by preheating the oven to 160°C (320°F) and greasing a 9-inch round cake pan. In a mixing bowl, beat 250 grams of softened cream cheese until smooth, then add 100 grams of sugar and continue beating until creamy. Gradually add three eggs, one at a time, beating well after each addition. Mix in a teaspoon of vanilla extract. In a separate bowl, sift 150 grams of all-purpose flour with a teaspoon of baking powder and a pinch of salt. Gradually fold the dry ingredients into the cream cheese mixture, alternating with 100 ml of milk until just combined. Pour the batter into the prepared pan and smooth the top. Bake for 45-50 minutes or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean. Allow the cake to cool in the pan for 10 minutes before transferring it to a wire rack to cool completely. For an optional topping, dust with powdered sugar or spread a thin layer of cream cheese frosting. This cake is light, creamy, and perfect for any occasion, providing a delightful balance of sweetness and tang.

Vanilla Cupcakes

To make delicious vanilla cupcakes, start by preheating the oven to 350°F (175°C) and lining a 12-cup muffin tin with cupcake liners. In a bowl, whisk together 1 1/2 cups of all-purpose flour, 1 1/2 teaspoons of baking powder, and a pinch of salt. In a separate bowl, beat 1/2 cup of softened unsalted butter and 1 cup of granulated sugar until creamy and smooth. Add 2 large eggs, one at a time, beating well after each addition. Stir in 2 teaspoons of pure vanilla extract. Gradually add the flour mixture, alternating with 1/2 cup of milk, beginning and ending with the flour mixture. Mix until just combined, being careful not to overmix. Divide the batter evenly among the cupcake liners, filling each about two-thirds full. Bake for 18-20 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean. Let the cupcakes cool in the pan for 5 minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely. For the frosting, beat 1/2 cup of softened unsalted butter until fluffy, then gradually add 2 cups of powdered sugar, 2 tablespoons of milk, and 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract, mixing until smooth. Frost the cooled cupcakes and enjoy!

Tiramisu Crepe Cake

To make a Tiramisu Crepe Cake, start by preparing the crepes. Whisk together 2 cups of flour, 2 tablespoons of sugar, and a pinch of salt in a bowl. In another bowl, mix 4 eggs, 2 cups of milk, 1 cup of water, and 4 tablespoons of melted butter. Gradually add the wet ingredients to the dry, stirring until smooth. Cook thin crepes in a lightly buttered skillet over medium heat, setting them aside to cool. For the filling, beat 1 cup of heavy cream with 1/4 cup of sugar and 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract until stiff peaks form. In another bowl, mix 1 cup of mascarpone cheese until smooth, then gently fold in the whipped cream. To assemble, place a crepe on a plate, spread a thin layer of mascarpone filling, and dust with cocoa powder. Repeat until all crepes are layered, topping the final one with filling and more cocoa powder. Refrigerate the cake for at least 2 hours to set. Before serving, dust with extra cocoa powder and optionally garnish with chocolate shavings or coffee beans for a rich finish. This dessert offers a delightful blend of creamy, coffee-flavored layers reminiscent of classic tiramisu.

Strawberry Cake

A strawberry cake combines the natural sweetness of strawberries with a moist, tender cake. Begin by preheating the oven to 350°F (175°C) and greasing two 9-inch round cake pans. In a bowl, mix 2 cups of flour, 2 teaspoons of baking powder, and a pinch of salt. In another bowl, beat 1 cup of softened butter and 1½ cups of sugar until fluffy, then add 4 eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition. Stir in 2 teaspoons of vanilla extract. Gradually add the dry ingredients, alternating with ½ cup of milk, beginning and ending with the dry mixture. Fold in 1 cup of finely chopped fresh strawberries. Divide the batter evenly between the prepared pans and bake for 25-30 minutes or until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean. Let the cakes cool in the pans for 10 minutes, then transfer them to a wire rack to cool completely. For the frosting, blend 2 cups of powdered sugar with ½ cup of softened butter, 2 tablespoons of pureed strawberries, and 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract until smooth. Layer the cakes with frosting and garnish with sliced strawberries for a beautiful finish.

Pineapple Coconut cake

For a delightful Pineapple Coconut Cake, start by preheating your oven to 350°F (175°C) and greasing a 9-inch round cake pan. In a large bowl, cream together 1 cup of unsalted butter and 2 cups of granulated sugar until light and fluffy. Add 4 large eggs, one at a time, mixing well after each addition. Stir in 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract and the zest of one lemon for a refreshing twist. In another bowl, combine 2 cups of all-purpose flour, 1 teaspoon of baking powder, and a pinch of salt. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet mixture, alternating with 1 cup of crushed pineapple (drained) and 1 cup of shredded coconut. Pour the batter into the prepared cake pan and smooth the top. Bake for 30-35 minutes or until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean. Let the cake cool in the pan for 10 minutes before transferring it to a wire rack. For added flavor, frost with a mixture of cream cheese and powdered sugar, then sprinkle with toasted coconut and some pineapple chunks on top. Enjoy your tropical treat that’s perfect for any occasion!
